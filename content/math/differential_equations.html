ODE Preliminaries

Linear Differential Equations

A differnetial equation is linear if it has the format

an(x)dnydxn+an−1(x)dn−1ydxn−1+...+a1(x)dydx+a0(x)y=F(x)a_n(x)\frac{d^n y}{dx^n}+a_{n-1}(x)\frac{d^{n-1}y}{dx^{n-1}}+...+a_1(x)\frac{dy}{dx} + a_0(x)y = F(x) an​(x)dxndny​+an−1​(x)dxn−1dn−1y​+...+a1​(x)dxdy​+a0​(x)y=F(x)

where an(x),...,a0(x)a_n(x),...,a_0(x)an​(x),...,a0​(x) and F(x)F(x)F(x) depend only on the independent variable x.

In other words, in order to be linear, a differential equation can only have 1 dedicated yyy term (not mingled with yyy's derivative terms), and the power of that yyy term must be 1.

A nonlinear differential equation is one that's not linear, e.g. y′′+y3=0y''+y^3 = 0y′′+y3=0

Explicit, Implicit, and General Solutions

For Initial Value Problems:

An Explicit Solution is a solution in the format of a function, e.g. y=f(x)y=f(x)y=f(x) with specified intervals.

An Implicit Solution is one in the format of an equation, (that may be manifested as different functions.)

For Generic Relationship Problems:

A General Solution to the differential equation represents all solutions. (Contains generic constants like CCC)

∂Fx∂y=∂Fy∂x\frac{\partial F_x}{\partial y}=\frac{\partial F_y}{\partial x} ∂y∂Fx​​=∂x∂Fy​​

In other words Mdx+Ndy=dF⇔Mi^+Nj^=∇FMdx+Ndy=dF \Leftrightarrow M\hat i+N\hat j=\nabla FMdx+Ndy=dF⇔Mi^+Nj^​=∇F

Integrating Factor

This is the last resort to make a non-linear, non-exact first-order ODE into an exact equation.

If the equation M(x,y)dx+N(x,y)dy=0M(x,y)dx+N(x,y)dy=0M(x,y)dx+N(x,y)dy=0 is not exact, and ∃μ(x,y)\exists \mu(x,y)∃μ(x,y) such that μMdx+μMdy=0\mu M dx + \mu M dy = 0μMdx+μMdy=0 is exact, then μ(x,y)\mu(x,y)μ(x,y) is called an Integrating Factor of the original equation.

For starters, to satisfy the exactness test:

∂∂y[μ(x,y)M(x,y)]=∂∂x[μ(x,y)N(x,y)]⇒M∂μ∂y−N∂μ∂x=(∂N∂x−∂M∂y)μ\frac{\partial}{\partial y}[\mu(x,y)M(x,y)]=\frac{\partial}{\partial x}[\mu(x,y)N(x,y)]\\ \Rightarrow M\frac{\partial \mu}{\partial y}-N\frac{\partial \mu}{\partial x} = \bigg(\frac{\partial N}{\partial x}-\frac{\partial M}{\partial y}\bigg)\mu ∂y∂​[μ(x,y)M(x,y)]=∂x∂​[μ(x,y)N(x,y)]⇒M∂y∂μ​−N∂x∂μ​=(∂x∂N​−∂y∂M​)μ

This partial differential equation turns out to be more difficult to solve than solving the original non-exact equation. As a result, we're only going to deal with integrating factors that depends only on one variable, so that the PDE could be reduced to a separable equation.

For example, when μ=μ(x)\mu = \mu(x)μ=μ(x):

dμdx=(∂M/∂y−∂N/∂xN)μ\frac{d\mu}{dx}=\bigg(\frac{\partial M/\partial y- \partial N / \partial x}{N}\bigg)\mu dxdμ​=(N∂M/∂y−∂N/∂x​)μ

∴\therefore∴ let H=(∂M/∂y−∂N/∂x)/NH = (\partial M/ \partial y - \partial N/ \partial x)/NH=(∂M/∂y−∂N/∂x)/N, H=H(x)⇔μ=μ(x)H=H(x)\Leftrightarrow \mu=\mu(x)H=H(x)⇔μ=μ(x). In other words, function HHH depends exclusively on xxx if and only if μ\muμ depends exclusively on x. (simply use proof by contradiction).
