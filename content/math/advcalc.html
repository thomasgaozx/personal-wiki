Projection of Curve onto Standard Plane

Projecting a curve onto x-z plane means getting rid of yyy component.

  1. Rearrange one of the equation as y(x,z)y(x,z)y(x,z)
  2. Substitute the other equation with y(x,z)y(x,z)y(x,z) and we have the projected curve.

Parameterization and Curve Plotting*

Usually a curve is given as the intersection of two surfaces.

  1. Draw the surfaces, clearly label the equation for the surface.
  2. Draw the intersection curve.
  3. On the intersection curve, indicate the direction of the curve as parameter ttt increases.

There is usually a constraint on the direction of the curve as ttt increases, a couple examples:

  • The curve is directed such that xxx increases along the curve
  • The curve is directed away from the origin in the first octant - the space where x,y,z∈[0,∞)x,y,z\in[0,\infty)x,y,z∈[0,∞)

Exam Advice

  1. Don't always try to solve the problem the "vector" way, don't be afraid to draw basic geometric diagram to obtain the values.
  2. For nasty vectors like unit binormal, it's often easier to compute the direction first before "shrinking length".
  3. For level curves (aka contour curves), one trick is to determine xxx when y→∞y\rightarrow\inftyy→∞ and y→0y\rightarrow0y→0, and determine yyy when x→∞x\rightarrow\inftyx→∞ and x→0x\rightarrow0x→0.
  4. For any continuity equation questions. Work out an expression and point out any inconsistencies or obvious discontinuities.
