Semiconductors

Intrinsic Semiconductors

  • Covalent bond: each pair of shared electrons.
  • Free electrons: thermal energy breaks some of the covalent bonds and free some electrons.
  • Holes: positively charged carriers that attracts an electron

The concentration of free electrons is denoted nnn, and the concentration of holes is denoted ppp.

Recombination is the process free electrons fill the holes. Thermal generation is the process that as temperature increases, more covalent bonds are broken and electron-hole pairs are generated.

In thermal equilibrium, the recombination rate is equal to the generation rate, and we have n=pn=pn=p.

PN Junction

Carrier-Depletion Region exist on both sides of the junction, with nnn side of of this region positively charged and ppp side negatively charged.

The charges on both sides of the depletion region cause an electric field EEE to be established across the region. Hence a potential difference results across the depletion region, with nnn side at a positive voltage relative to the ppp side. This voltage drop acts as a barrier that has to be overcome for holes to diffuse into the nnn region adn electrons to diffuse into the ppp region. This voltage V0V_0V0​ is called junction built-in voltage, for typical diodes, V0=0.7V_0=0.7V0​=0.7.

Note that when pnpnpn junction terminals are open, the voltage measured between the terminals will be zero. In other words, the voltage V0V_0V0​ across the depletion region does not appear between the junction terminals. This is because of the contact voltages existing at the metal semiconductor junctions at the terminals.which counter and exactly balance the barrier voltage.

Diodes

Ideal Diode

If a negative voltage is applied to the diode, no current flows and the diode behaves as an open circuit. Diodes operated in this mode are said to be reverse biased, or operated in the reverse direction. An ideal diode has zero current when operated in the reverse direction and is said to be cut off, or simply off.

On the other hand, if a positive current is applied to the ideal diode, zero voltage drop appears across the diode. In other words, the ideal diode behaves as a short circuit in the forward direction; it passes any current with zero voltage drop. A forward-biased diode is said to be turned on, or simply on.

  • Anode: positive terminal
  • Cathode: negative terminal

MOS Field-Effect Transistors

Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor Field-Effect Transistor (MOSFET) has

  • 2 heavily n-doped regions: Source and Drain
  • Gate electrode: metal deposited on top of the silicon-oxide layer
  • Body: metal contacts made to the p-substrate region

The four terminals:

  • Gate Terminal GGG
  • Source Terminal SSS (generally kept grounded)
  • Drain Terminal DDD
  • Body Terminal BBB

When there is a positive voltage applied to gate, the gate voltage attracts free electrons from source. When a sufficient number of electrons accumulate near the surface of the substrate under the gate, an n region is in effect created in the p-substrate, connecting the source and drain regions. The induced n-region thus forms a channel for current flow from drain to source, hence the name n-channel MOSFET, NMOS.

General Structure of CMOS Logic

A CMOS logic circuit is in effect an extension of the CMOS inverter. The inverter consists of an NMOS pull-down transistor and a PMOS pull-up transistor. The CMOS logic gate consists of two networks: the pull-down network (PDN) using NMOS, and the pull-up network (PUN) using PMOS.
